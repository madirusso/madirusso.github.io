the peristomal skin barrier sensor

The peristomal skin barrier sensor is a flexible apparatus that detects the location and the angle of bend through the use of copper electrodes, which are placed at varying angles within the sensor teeth. When an electrode pair makes contact after a bend, the data collection and processing algorithm is able to determine the angle and location of the bend. This application would ideally be extended for use on an ostomy skin barrier to monitor its surface deformation on a human body. Both a “normally-opened” and “normally-closed” sensor variation has been created in order to account for the various movements a person undergoes during everyday activities.
